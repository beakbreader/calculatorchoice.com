What is Simple Interest?

Simple interest is calculated only on the initial principal, making it straightforward and predictable. It doesn't compound over time.

Formula

A = P(1 + rt)
Where:
P = principal amount
r = annual interest rate (decimal)
t = number of years
A = amount after time

Example

Investing $1,000 at an annual rate of 5% for 10 years will grow to $1,500 with $500 interest earned.
