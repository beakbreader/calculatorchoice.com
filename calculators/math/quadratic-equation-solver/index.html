    How to Solve a Quadratic Equation

    The standard quadratic form ax² + bx + c = 0 can be solved using the quadratic formula:

    x = [-b ± √(b² − 4ac)] / 2a

    The discriminant (b² - 4ac) tells you whether the roots are real or complex.
